Competation Rules

To be eligible to enter the Competition, you must be:

    a) Participation is open to students of the Institute of Public Higher Education (IPTA) and the Institute of Private Higher Education (IPTS) who are Malaysian citizens. However, each group is allowed to be composed of one participant consisting of non-IPTA and IPTS students.

    b) Participation is FREE.

    c) Participation is in groups of 3 to 5 people

    d) Registration of entries is through the official portal.

    e) Products developed should be from scratch, genuine and not tied to any Intellectual Property (Intellectual Property).

    f) Product development must be within the stipulated time period. Product submissions made after expiration will not be evaluated by the panel of evaluators.

    g) Participants are required to use at least one (1) key data set supplied by the secretariat or can be downloaded from the DOSM virtual Datathon Portal (DvD).

    h) The use of other data as secondary data is encouraged.

    i) The secondary data source suggested can be referenced at eStatistik, Statistics Data Warehouse (StatsDW), Malaysian Open Data etc.

    j) Development using open source applications(open source)is encouraged.

    k) Participants are required to provide the data set used ini machine-readableformat to the organizers.

    l) Participants must submit a report of the product developed by uploading it into the DOSM Virtual Datathon (DvD 2020) Portal.

    m) The report format will be provided and included when the Participant downloads the dataset at the beginning of the competition which include:

  • i. Product development proposal;
  • ii. Product / application developed (source file);
  • iii. Data sets used (open data sets and data sets from other sources).

      • n) The final product produced must be in the form of web based or mobile application and can be accessed until the evaluation has been made.

      o) Products that have been developed are government-owned and cannot be claimed by the Participant or the Participant Representative.

      p) Participants are required to use their own ICT hardware and software throughout the competition period.

      q) The Organizer reserves the right to amend the terms and conditions without having to inform the participants in advance.

      r) The Organizer reserves the right to cancel the entry if there is a violation of the conditions.

      s) The Organizer is not responsible for any incidents, health problems, losses, damages and losses suffered while participating in the program.

      t) The panel's evaluation decision is FINAL and no objection will be entertained.

      u) The final product description and presentation are in English.

    Competition Assessment

    1. 1. Final Evaluation Session is based on the submission of projects submitted and will be conducted on 4th December 2020.

    2. 2. The final project presentation for the 10 shortlisted entries will be conducted in a video conference (Presentation Schedule will be given later).

    3. 3. The Organizer reserves the right to modify or enhance the mobile application for the purpose of use by any Government agency where appropriate.

    4. 4. The panel evaluation results are FINAL and differences of opinion will not be entertained.

    Assessment Criteria

    01 FUNCTIONAL
    Mobile applications can work well and meet the mandatory specifications set out in the entry requirements

    02 VALIDITY
    The applications produced are relevant in solving problems as well as providing benefits and impact to the Government and the people.

    03 INTEGRITY
    The application is to use a reliable data source and produce accurate, quality, integrity and attractive data output.

    04 CREDIBILITY
    Efficient and clear in presenting the applications that have been developed and showing the spirit of teamwork.

    05 POTENTIALITY
    The application is quality, innovative, interactive, user friendly and can be commercialized and developed in the future.

    Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

    • a) What is DvD 2020?
      DOSM Virtual Datathon 2020 (DvD 2020) is a datathon competition that brings together the brightest minds in the data science world in Malaysia to showcase their data analytics capabilities by developing thought-provoking business solutions.

    • b) How to apply?
      Participant can register online through this link. You will be directed to a form where you will complete all the detail information needed.

    • c) Is there any age limit to participate?
      You can join the competition if you are above 18 years old and no age limitation.

    • d) Who can join?
      All IPTA/IPTS students and working professionals. Registration only opens to teams of minimum 3 people and maximum 5 people. We wonâ€™t accept individual participations.

    • f) Is the datathon free to enter?
      Yes, registration to participate in the Datathon is free!

    • g) Do I need to be a data scientist or an advanced analytics expert to enter?
      Not necessarily, the datathon is open to people of all skill sets and capabilities. However, it ia advisable to have strong analytics capability among team members to generate the insights.

    • h) What kind of software/tools will the participants be using?
      You can use any tools available on your laptop including open sources software such as R, phyton, power BI, etc.

    • i) Who to contact for any enquiries?
      You can email your enquiries to dosmdatathon@dosm.gov.my

    • j) Where is the venue of this competition?
      This competition is held virtually and at your own space.

    • k) Do I need to attend the final pitches on 9 December 2020?
      Only shortlisted candidates will do the final pitches which also virtually through video conference.

    • l) How many participants will present the project via video conference?
      Only the top 10 best projects shortlisted.

    • m) Will the laptop be provided?
      No, all the data modelling/data preparation/feature engineering needs to be done using your own laptop.

    • n) Is there any personal documents need to be submitted for registration?
      Yes, please get ready a copy of your indentification card for verification purpose during registration.

    • o) What are the prizes?
      The Champion, 1st runner up, and 2nd runner up team will be awarded a prize of MYR3000, MYR2000, and MYR1000 respectively.

    • p) What are the eligibility criteria to join DvD 2020?
      We are looking for students and working individuals from all backgrounds including data scientists, data engineers, programmers, data community avtivists, computer engineers, IT professionals, and business consultants.

    • q) Can someone use my name and participate in the competition?
      No, we do not tolerate any kind of mispresentation.

    • r) How can I form a team to participate?
      You need to create your own team before registration.

    • s) Can a non-citizen join this competition?
      No.

    • t) What data do we get?
      The compulsory data to be used are Price Intelligence (PI), Trade in Malaysia, Geospatial and StatsDW@DOSM.

    • u) Will the data be explained?
      Yes, the organizer will provide an overview of the data structure and it is up to your team to analyse data and generate insights.

    • v) Can we use additional data?
      Yes, you can use any public data from reliable source.
